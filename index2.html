About Al-Farabi

Al-Farabi (872–950), also known as Alpharabius, was a renowned philosopher, scientist, and scholar of the Islamic Golden Age. Born in Farab (modern Kazakhstan), he contributed greatly to philosophy, logic, music, and political theory. Often called the "Second Teacher" after Aristotle, his works influenced both Islamic and European thought.

Achievements

  • Major contributions to philosophy and logic
  • Developed influential works in political theory
  • Founded key principles in music theory
  • Wrote commentaries on Aristotle’s works
